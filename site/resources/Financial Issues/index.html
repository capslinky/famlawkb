    Financial Issues¶

    üîç At a Glance¶

    Quick Summary: Family law cases involve many financial issues - from disclosing assets to getting temporary support, paying attorney fees, and enforcing orders.

    Who This Affects: Everyone going through divorce or family court must deal with financial disclosure and may need temporary financial help.

    Key Point: Full financial disclosure is mandatory. Hiding assets or income has serious consequences.

    ‚ö° Key Takeaways¶

    • üìã Must disclose all finances within 40 days
    • üí∞ Can get temporary support immediately
    • ‚öñÔ∏è Court can order spouse to pay your attorney
    • üî® Many tools to collect unpaid support
    • üö´ Bankruptcy won't eliminate support debts
    • üîç Hidden assets can be discovered
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Disclosure violations = sanctions

    üìä Financial Disclosure¶

    What You Must Disclose¶

    Due: Within 40 days of Response

    Income Documents üíµ: - Tax returns (3 years) - W-2s and 1099s (3 years) - Pay stubs (6 months) - Business profit/loss - Unemployment/disability - All other income

    Asset Documents üè†: - Bank statements (12 months) - Investment accounts - Retirement accounts - Real estate deeds - Vehicle titles - Business interests

    Debt Documents üí≥: - Credit card statements - Loan documents - Mortgage statements - Medical bills - Student loans - All other debts

    Affidavit of Financial Information¶

    Must Include: 1. Monthly income - All sources 2. Monthly expenses - Actual costs 3. Assets - Everything you own 4. Debts - Everything you owe 5. Children's expenses - Detailed

    Common Mistakes ‚ùå: - Forgetting accounts - Underreporting income - Inflating expenses - Missing signature - Not updating changes

    Consequences of Not Disclosing¶

    What Happens: - ‚ö†Ô∏è Evidence thrown out - üí∞ Pay other side's attorney - üö´ Default judgment - ‚öñÔ∏è Contempt of court - üîí Possible jail time

    Sanctions Get Worse: 1. First time: Warning 2. Continued: Financial penalties 3. Willful: Criminal charges

    üí∞ Temporary Support¶

    What You Can Get Now¶

    While Case Pending:

    1. Child Support üë∂ - Starts immediately - Uses guideline calculator - Includes medical/childcare - Direct from paycheck

    2. Spousal Support üíë - Maintains status quo - Based on need - Temporary only - Can be modified

    3. Exclusive Use üè† - Stay in house - Use car - Access accounts - Keep insurance

    4. Bill Payment üì± - Mortgage/rent - Utilities - Insurance - Minimum credit cards

    Emergency Orders¶

    When Available üö®: - Money being hidden - Kids need immediate help - Utilities being shut off - Insurance cancelled - True emergency

    How to Get: 1. File emergency motion 2. Explain urgent need 3. Try to notify other side 4. Judge reviews quickly 5. Order lasts 10-15 days

    What to File¶

    Documents Needed: - ‚úì Motion for Temporary Orders - ‚úì Financial Affidavit - ‚úì Income proof - ‚úì Expense list - ‚úì Proposed order

    Tip: Be realistic about expenses

    ‚öñÔ∏è Attorney Fees¶

    Can Spouse Pay My Attorney?¶

    Yes, if: - They earn more - You can't afford attorney - They control assets - They're being unreasonable

    When Court Orders Fees¶

    During Case (Interim): - Level playing field - Based on need - Monthly payments OK - Can request anytime

    End of Case (Final): - Who "won" matters - Bad behavior costs more - Reasonableness of positions - Total income difference

    On Appeal: - Separate request - Success matters - Ability at time of appeal

    How Much?¶

    Court Considers: - ‚úì Actual attorney bills - ‚úì Reasonable rates - ‚úì Necessary work - ‚úì Local standards - ‚úì Case complexity

    Typical Awards: - Partial payment common - Full fees if big disparity - Bad faith = more fees - Percentage of total

    What You Need to Show¶

    Your Attorney Files: 1. Fee Affidavit - Total charged - Amount paid - Balance due - Why reasonable

    1. Detailed Bills
    2. Time entries
    3. Who did what
    4. Hourly rates

    5. Costs itemized

    6. Financial Comparison

    7. Your income/assets
    8. Their income/assets
    9. Ability to pay

    Expert Witness Fees¶

    Who Pays for: - Custody evaluator - Business appraiser - Real estate appraiser - Vocational expert

    Usually: Split or higher earner pays

    üî® Collecting Support¶

    If They Won't Pay¶

    Automatic Tools:

    1. Wage Garnishment üí∞ - Direct from paycheck - Employer must comply - Up to 50-65% of pay - Includes back support

    2. Bank Levy üè¶ - Freeze accounts - Take money owed - Any bank in Arizona - Some funds protected

    3. Property Lien üè† - Automatic for support - Clouds title - Must pay to sell - Can foreclose

    4. Tax Refund üìã - Federal and state - Automatic intercept - Applied to arrears - Notice sent first

    License Suspension¶

    If $5,000+ Behind:

    Professional Licenses üëî: - Medical/dental - Law license - Real estate - Contractor - Business

    Driver's License üöó: - 30-day warning - Can get restricted - Pay to reinstate - Very effective

    Other Collection Tools¶

    Credit Reporting üìä: - Automatic if $1,000+ - All three bureaus - Hurts credit score - Stays until paid

    Passport Denial ‚úàÔ∏è: - If $2,500+ owed - Can't get/renew - International travel blocked

    Criminal Charges üöî: - Willful non-payment - Class 6 felony possible - Rarely used - Last resort

    ‚öñÔ∏è Contempt of Court¶

    When Used¶

    For Willful Violations: - Won't pay support - Ignoring court orders - Hiding assets - Denying parenting time - Not providing disclosure

    What Court Must Find¶

    To Hold in Contempt: 1. ‚úì Valid order exists 2. ‚úì Person knew about it 3. ‚úì Had ability to comply 4. ‚úì Willfully disobeyed

    Key: "Ability" matters most

    Consequences¶

    Civil Contempt (Most Common): - Jail until comply - "Keys in pocket" - Pay attorney fees - Make-up time - Purge conditions

    Criminal Contempt (Rare): - Fixed jail sentence - Criminal record - Can't purge - Punishment focused

    Common Defenses¶

    Valid Excuses: - Lost job (prove it) - Medical emergency - In jail/hospital - Truly can't comply - Order unclear

    NOT Excuses: - "Forgot" - "Too busy" - "Don't agree" - "It's unfair"

    How It Works¶

    1. File Motion
    2. List violations
    3. Attach evidence

    4. Request remedies

    5. Serve Papers

    6. Personal service

    7. Proper notice

    8. Hearing

    9. Present evidence
    10. They explain

    11. Judge decides

    12. If Found in Contempt

    13. Immediate custody possible
    14. Set purge amount
    15. Pay to get out

    üí∏ Bankruptcy Issues¶

    What Bankruptcy CAN'T Do¶

    Never Discharged üö´: - Child support - Spousal maintenance - Support arrears - Attorney fees for support

    Still Must Pay: - During bankruptcy - After bankruptcy - Forever until paid - Interest continues

    What Bankruptcy CAN Do¶

    Chapter 7 (Liquidation): - Some property debts - Credit card debts - Medical bills - BUT support continues

    Chapter 13 (Payment Plan): - 3-5 year plan - Support gets priority - Must stay current - Catch up arrears

    Important Points¶

    Automatic Stay: - Doesn't stop support collection - Wage garnishment continues - Can still enforce - Criminal prosecution OK

    Strategy Issues: - Timing matters - How debts labeled - Hold harmless clauses - Creditor rights

    If Spouse Files: - Keep collecting support - May affect property division - Watch for fraud - Get bankruptcy attorney

    üîç Finding Hidden Assets¶

    Red Flags to Watch¶

    Behavior Changes üö©: - Suddenly "broke" - Secret accounts - Cash lifestyle - New girlfriend/boyfriend - Offshore talk - Cryptocurrency interest

    Document Clues üìÑ: - Missing statements - Altered records - Unexplained deposits - Large withdrawals - Business "loans" - Delayed income

    How to Find Assets¶

    Discovery Tools:

    1. Interrogatories ‚ùì - Written questions - Under oath answers - Asset locations - Income sources

    2. Document Requests üìÅ - All financial records - Electronic files - Emails/texts - Business records

    3. Depositions üé§ - Question under oath - Court reporter present - Can't refuse to answer - Follow up allowed

    4. Subpoenas üìÆ - Banks directly - Employers - Business partners - Anyone with info

    Forensic Accounting¶

    What They Do: - Trace money flows - Find hidden accounts - Value businesses - Analyze lifestyle - Spot fraud

    When Worth It: - Complex finances - Business owner - High assets - Clear deception - Cost vs. benefit

    Common Hiding Places¶

    Look For: - üí∞ Cash businesses - üè¶ New accounts - üë• Friend/family "loans" - üè¢ Fake employees - üìä Delayed bonuses - üíé Physical assets - üåê Online accounts - ü™ô Cryptocurrency

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes¶

    Disclosure Mistakes¶

    1. Incomplete Disclosure ‚ùå - Forgetting accounts - Missing assets - Old 401(k)s - Side income

    Fix: List everything, even if unsure

    2. False Information ‚ùå - Lying about income - Hiding assets - Fake expenses - Altered documents

    Fix: Tell truth - always discovered

    3. Late Disclosure ‚ùå - Missing 40-day deadline - Not updating changes - Waiting for reminders

    Fix: Calendar deadline immediately

    Support Collection Mistakes¶

    4. Waiting Too Long ‚ùå - Letting arrears build - Not enforcing immediately - Hoping they'll pay

    Fix: Act on first missed payment

    5. Not Using All Tools ‚ùå - Only trying garnishment - Giving up easily - Not reporting to credit

    Fix: Use every collection method

    6. Poor Record Keeping ‚ùå - No payment tracking - Lost receipts - Informal payments

    Fix: Document everything

    ‚ùì Common Questions¶

    Can I get support if spouse controls money?¶

    Yes! File for temporary orders: - Immediate relief available - Emergency orders possible - Exclusive use of accounts - Court can freeze assets

    What if I can't afford an attorney?¶

    Options: - Request attorney fees from spouse - Payment plans with attorney - Limited scope representation - Legal aid if qualify - Court can order spouse to pay

    Will bankruptcy eliminate support?¶

    No - Support is forever: - Child support survives - Spousal maintenance too - Collection continues - Some property debts discharged

    How do I find hidden money?¶

    Use these tools: - Subpoena banks - Review tax returns - Check lifestyle vs income - Hire forensic accountant - Look for red flags

    What if they won't pay support?¶

    Many enforcement tools: - Wage garnishment automatic - Seize bank accounts - Suspend licenses - Report to credit bureaus - Contempt with jail

    Is my inheritance separate?¶

    Yes, if: - Kept separate account - Never commingled - No spouse name added - Used for separate property

    Can they empty accounts?¶

    No - Preliminary injunction prevents: - Automatically issued - Freezes status quo - Violations = contempt - Court can reverse transfers

    When is contempt used?¶

    For: - Willful non-payment - Violating orders - Hiding assets - Not disclosing

    Must prove: Ability to comply

    Do I need receipts?¶

    Yes! Keep proof of: - All payments made - All payments received - How paid (check #, etc) - When paid - Written agreements

    Why: Disputes always arise
